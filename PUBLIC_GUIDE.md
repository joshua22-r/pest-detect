# BioGuard AI - Complete User Guide

Welcome to BioGuard AI, your comprehensive AI-powered health detection system for both plants and livestock!

## 🌟 Overview

BioGuard AI uses advanced artificial intelligence to instantly detect diseases, pests, and health issues in:
- **🌿 Plants** - Detect fungal diseases, bacterial infections, viral diseases, and pest damage
- **🐄 Livestock** - Identify health conditions, pest infestations, and skin diseases in animals

## 🚀 Getting Started

### 1. Create Your Account
- Click "Get Started Free" on the homepage
- Enter your email, name, and create a secure password
- Verify your email address
- You're ready to start detecting!

### 2. Understanding the Dashboard
- **Home**: Overview of your scan history and quick stats
- **Detect & Analyze**: Upload images for instant AI analysis
- **History**: View all your previous scans with filtering options

## 📸 How to Use Detection

### Step 1: Choose Subject Type
Select whether you want to analyze:
- **🌿 Plant** - For crops, flowers, vegetables, or houseplants
- **🐄 Livestock** - For cattle, sheep, goats, horses, pigs, or poultry

### Step 2: Upload Image
Choose one of three methods:
1. **Drag & Drop** - Drag an image directly onto the upload area
2. **Click to Browse** - Select from your device files
3. **Take Photo** - Capture a live photo from your camera (mobile)

### Step 3: Get Instant Analysis
The AI analyzes your image within seconds and provides:
- **Disease/Condition Name** - Exact identification of what's detected
- **Confidence Score** - How certain the AI is (0-100%)
- **Severity Level** - Low, Medium, or High severity rating
- **Affected Species** - Which plants or animals typically get this condition
- **Treatment Recommendations** - Specific steps to address the issue
- **Prevention Tips** - How to prevent it from happening again

### Step 4: Save to History
Click "Save to History" to keep a record of the scan for future reference

## 📋 Plant Disease Detection

### Supported Plant Diseases

**Fungal Diseases:**
- Powdery Mildew - White powdery coating on leaves
- Leaf Spot - Brown/black spots on foliage
- Rust - Orange/rust-colored patches

**Prevention Tips:**
- Water at the base of plants, not on leaves
- Ensure good air circulation
- Space plants adequately
- Remove infected leaves promptly
- Use organic or synthetic fungicides as recommended

### Tips for Best Plant Photos
✓ Use clear, well-lit photos
✓ Focus on affected areas (leaves, stems, flowers)
✓ Avoid shadows and reflections
✓ Get close enough to see details
✓ Supported formats: JPG, PNG, WebP (Max 5MB)

## 🐄 Livestock Health Detection

### Supported Animal Conditions

**Pest Infestations:**
- Tick Infestation - Parasitic ticks on skin/coat
- Mite Infestation - Microscopic parasites causing itching
- Fly Infestations - Botflies, warble flies, etc.

**Skin Conditions:**
- Bacterial Infections - Open sores, pustules
- Fungal Infections - Ring worm, mange-like symptoms
- Skin Lesions - Wounds, abrasions, swelling

**General Health Issues:**
- Lameness - Limping or difficulty walking
- Eye/Nasal Discharge - Signs of illness
- Coat/Hoof Quality Issues - Poor nutrition or health indicators

### Supported Livestock Species
- Cattle (Beef & Dairy)
- Sheep & Lambs
- Goats & Kids
- Horses & Equines
- Pigs & Piglets
- Chickens, Ducks, Turkeys, and Poultry

### Tips for Best Livestock Photos
✓ Capture clear, close-up images of the affected area
✓ Ensure good lighting to show coat/skin condition
✓ Include visible pests, wounds, or abnormalities
✓ Avoid motion blur (animal standing still preferred)
✓ Take multiple angles if possible
✓ Supported formats: JPG, PNG, WebP (Max 5MB)

## 🎯 Best Practices

### For Accurate Detection

**Plant Photography:**
1. Take photos in natural daylight
2. Focus on the most affected areas
3. Show both healthy and affected parts for comparison
4. Include multiple angles if the disease is widespread
5. Avoid photos with water droplets or dirt

**Livestock Photography:**
1. Restrain the animal safely for clear photos
2. Use natural or bright artificial light
3. Shave/clean the area if examining skin conditions
4. Get multiple close-ups of the affected region
5. Include the animal's overall condition if relevant

### Interpreting Results

**Confidence Score:**
- **90-100%** - Very reliable diagnosis
- **75-89%** - Good confidence, fairly reliable
- **60-74%** - Moderate confidence, consider other factors
- **Below 60%** - Lower confidence, get professional confirmation

**Severity Levels:**
- **Low** - Monitor closely, preventive measures recommended
- **Medium** - Treat soon to prevent spread/worsening
- **High** - Immediate treatment required, consult professional

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

### Image Upload Issues
- Ensure file is JPG, PNG, or WebP format
- File size must be under 5MB
- Try refreshing the page and uploading again
- Check your internet connection

### Inaccurate Detection
- Ensure image is clear and well-lit
- Focus on the affected area
- Avoid shadows and reflections
- Take multiple photos from different angles
- Consult a professional for confirmation

### Account Issues
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try a different browser
- Reset your password if needed
- Contact support if problems persist
